# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to making participation in the factlog community a respectful,
harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size,
disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression,
level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal
appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language.
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.
- Focusing criticism on ideas, code, documentation, and behavior rather than on
  people.
- Showing empathy toward other community members.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discriminatory language or imagery.
- Personal attacks, insults, trolling, or sustained disruption of discussions.
- Publishing other people's private information without explicit permission.
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances.
- Any other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including GitHub issues,
pull requests, discussions, documentation, community chat, and any other public
or private forum where someone represents the project.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it to the project
maintainers. If a public issue or pull request is appropriate, open one with the
relevant context. If the report contains sensitive information, contact a
maintainer privately instead.

Reports should include enough detail for maintainers to understand what
happened, such as links, screenshots, dates, participants, and any prior
communication. Maintainers will respect confidentiality as much as possible
while investigating and responding.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards of acceptable behavior and
may take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to behavior that
violates this Code of Conduct.

Enforcement actions may include:

- A private or public warning.
- Editing, hiding, or deleting comments, commits, issues, pull requests, or
  other contributions.
- Temporary or permanent limitation of participation in project spaces.
- Reporting conduct to platform administrators or other appropriate parties when
  necessary.

Maintainers who are involved in a reported incident should recuse themselves
from handling that report when practical.
